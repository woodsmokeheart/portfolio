Testing Kafka-based systems feels intimidating until you split the problem into
small, observable pieces. The broker itself is rarely the thing you need to
verify — what you actually care about is whether your producers emit the right
events and your consumers react to them correctly.

## Start from the contract

Treat every topic as a contract. Pin down the message schema, the partition key,
and the ordering guarantees you depend on. Once those are explicit, most "flaky
Kafka tests" turn out to be ordering or idempotency assumptions that were never
written down.

## Make consumers observable

The fastest feedback loop is a test consumer that asserts on the events your
service produces. Spin up an ephemeral topic, push a known payload through the
producer, and assert on what lands on the other side. No mocking the broker, no
guessing.

## Keep it boring

Boring tests survive refactors. Prefer real (or test-container) brokers for
integration coverage and reserve mocks for the thin edges where speed matters
more than fidelity.
